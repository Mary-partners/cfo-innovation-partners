Who We Are

Climate Intelligence, Delivered at the Farm Level

CFO Innovation Partners is a Kenya-based climate risk and advisory firm specializing in climate risk assessment, climate finance advisory, and sustainability compliance. We help organizations quantify physical and transition climate risks using TCFD-aligned frameworks.

We are now channeling this institutional-grade climate risk capability toward smallholder agriculture — building the AgriClimate Intelligence Platform (ACIP) to deliver AI-powered, geospatially-enabled climate risk intelligence directly to farmers and extension agents across Sub-Saharan Africa.

The Challenge

The Climate Intelligence Gap

While institutional climate data and risk analytics have grown exponentially, the 33+ million farming households most vulnerable to climate shocks remain disconnected from this intelligence.

60% of Africa's workforce depends on agriculture
80% of food consumed is produced by smallholders
<10% of farmers reached by current extension services
132M additional people at risk of extreme poverty by 2030
Our Solution

AgriClimate Intelligence Platform (ACIP)

An AI-powered climate risk intelligence system that translates complex climate science into actionable, farm-level agroecological guidance delivered through channels smallholder farmers already use.

Climate Risk Engine

Processes Sentinel-2 satellite imagery and CHIRPS rainfall data to generate localized climate risk scores at the ward level using AI and geospatial analysis.

  • Sentinel-2 & CHIRPS data integration
  • Ward-level risk scoring
  • 10-day forecast modeling

USSD / SMS / IVR Delivery

Farmers dial a simple code on any feature phone to receive climate risk scores and agroecological recommendations in local languages.

  • Works on feature phones
  • Swahili & Kinyarwanda
  • Voice for low-literacy users

Extension Agent App

Offline-first Android app enabling extension agents to serve 3x more farmers with ACIP-guided, GPS-enabled, data-driven advisory visits.

  • Offline-first design
  • GPS-enabled tracking
  • 3x farmer reach

Agroecological Advisory Module

Crop-climate suitability mapping, intercropping recommendations, soil conservation timing, and pest/disease early warnings calibrated to climate predictions.

  • Crop-specific guidance
  • Indigenous knowledge integration
  • Pest & disease alerts
M4D Challenge Tracks

Building Resilient Food Systems

Our solution addresses critical challenge tracks in the M4D Open Innovation Challenge, with particular focus on Digital Extension and Fragility.

01

Digital Extension

ACIP transforms agricultural extension by equipping agents with AI-powered tools and connecting farmers directly to localized climate intelligence via USSD, SMS, and IVR — reaching the 90%+ of farmers currently underserved by traditional extension.

Primary Focus
02

Fragility

Climate shocks are a primary driver of food system fragility. ACIP builds resilience by providing early warning systems, climate-adaptive farming recommendations, and data infrastructure that strengthens community-level decision making in vulnerable regions.

Primary Focus
03

Agroecology

Our Agroecological Advisory Module delivers climate-calibrated sustainable farming practices — from intercropping to soil conservation — enabling transitions to resilient agroecological systems.

04

AI for Good

Cross-cutting use of AI and geospatial analysis to process satellite imagery, climate models, and ground-truth farmer data into actionable intelligence that creates positive impact.

Roadmap

Phased Implementation Strategy

Each phase builds on the previous one, generating the evidence and capabilities needed to unlock the next stage of impact.

0

Validation & Co-Design

Months 1–3

Validate the ACIP concept through M4D bootcamp mentorship from IFAD, ESA, and CGIAR. Co-design pilot approach with farming communities in Makueni County, Kenya.

30 farmer interviews 5 extension agents KALRO partnership
1

MVP & Field Testing

Months 4–8 · $10,000

Build and deploy the MVP Climate Risk Engine in Makueni County. USSD-based climate risk alerts to 2,000 farmers through 20 extension agents.

2

Pilot Expansion

Months 9–18 · $100,000

Scale to 3 districts across Kenya and Tanzania. Full Agroecological Advisory Module, extension agent app, IVR voice delivery, and rigorous impact evaluation.

Scale & Sustainability

How ACIP Works

01

Satellite Data Ingestion

Sentinel-2 imagery, CHIRPS rainfall, and ERA5 climate data are continuously ingested and processed.

02

AI Risk Analysis

Machine learning models generate localized climate risk scores and crop-specific agroecological recommendations.

03

Farmer Delivery

04

Ground-Truth Feedback

Farmer-reported outcomes validate and improve AI models, creating a continuous learning loop.
